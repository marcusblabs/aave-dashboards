The four APR engines

every vault's yield decomposes into these; chips reappear per row below
Native yield — the collateral token itself pays (staking, funding); structural while the source holds. Swap fees — traders pay both LP sides: income on the collateral, rebate on the debt; volume-dependent, drifts intraday ⇅. Base carry — the plain lend-minus-borrow spread; usually thin on correlated pairs. Incentives — Merkl campaigns subsidize the APR; real cash flow, but temporary.

Standing question — is LST debt the problem?

a counterfactual across every wstETH-borrowing vault, recomputed live
wstETH-debt question LST debt costs less than it looks D ~+1 to +1.9% at max leverage▼

Swapping wstETH debt for plain ETH would improve these loops by only ~+1 to +1.9% at their max leverage — mostly arbitraged away: the 2.21% staking drag is priced into wstETH's near-zero borrow rate (0.13%), so the true ETH-terms costs nearly cancel (2.34% vs 2.25%). The real lever is the collateral-vs-debt yield gap, not the LST-ness of the debt.
